CKD Progression Stages

GFR: 90+

Stage 1: Initial Damage

Kidneys function normally but physical markers of damage are present. Proactive monitoring begins here.

GFR: 60-89

Stage 2: Mild Decline

Evidence of mild kidney damage and slight reduction in filtration efficiency.

GFR: 30-59

Stage 3 (A/B): Moderate Decline

A critical transition phase where symptoms may start to appear. Split into 3a (mild-moderate) and 3b (moderate-severe).

GFR: 15-29

Stage 4: Severe Decline

Severely impaired kidney function. Preparation for renal replacement therapy is often discussed.

GFR: < 15

Stage 5: Kidney Failure

End-stage renal disease (ESRD). Kidneys have stopped working or are close to failing.

Management & Treatment

Primary Protocols
  • Blood Pressure: ACE inhibitors or ARBs help protect kidney vasculature.
  • Phosphate Binders: Assist in the elimination of dietary phosphate.
  • Erythropoietin: Stimulates red blood cell production to fight anemia.
  • Dietary Shift: Careful regulation of protein, sodium, and potassium.
Renal Replacement

In advanced Stage 5 cases, the following therapies are essential:

Hemodialysis

Mechanical blood filtration, typically 3x per week in-clinic.

Transplantation

Surgical replacement. Living donor (fastest) or Deceased donor (3-5 years average wait).
